About Mumtaz Alam Bazai

Mumtaz Alam Bazai is a Pakistani advocate, human rights lawyer, youth leader, and social activist from Balochistan. He is committed to justice, rule of law, and youth empowerment. Mumtaz is widely recognized for his work in civil, criminal, and human rights law, alongside his leadership roles in youth and social initiatives.

Born on 11 August 2000 in kili Zdoon, Tehsil Nana Sahib, District Pishin, Balochistan, Pakistan. He belongs to the Pashtoon tribe (Kakar caste, Bazai sub-caste). He is the eldest of nine siblings. His father, Dr. Ameer Muhammad, is a neurosurgeon, and his mother is a homemaker.

From early schooling in his village to scholarship-based education at Pak-Turk Maarif International Schools, Mumtaz demonstrated excellence in academics, sports, and multilingual communication. Fluent in Pashto, Urdu, English, and Turkish, he combines intellectual capability with social engagement and discipline.

Education

Mumtaz completed his intermediate education at Pak-Turk Maarif International Schools & Colleges in 2019. He then pursued higher education in law and political science, achieving first-division marks across all degrees:

  • LLB (Hons.) – University Law College (2024)
  • MA Political Science – University of Balochistan (2024)
  • BA (Arts) – University of Balochistan (2022)
  • Intermediate – Pak-Turk Maarif International Schools & Colleges (2019)

Legal Career

Mumtaz formally entered the legal profession in 2025. He trained under Advocate Abdul Zahir Kakar at Iqra Law Associates, Quetta, gaining experience in civil, criminal, and human rights law. On 2 December 2025, he received his license to practice before the trial courts of Pakistan (Bar Council Reg. No. 14/2025).

He actively takes pro bono cases, representing marginalized communities, and is known for his commitment to justice and legal empowerment in high-risk regions.

Leadership & Social Impact

Parallel to his legal career, Mumtaz has demonstrated a lifelong dedication to volunteering, youth leadership, and social activism:

  • Curator – Global Shapers Community Quetta (World Economic Forum)
  • Social Media Head – Global Shapers Community Quetta (2022–2024)
  • Cabinet Member – General Assembly Balochistan (2025)
  • Legal Advisor & Executive Member – Human Rights Council of Balochistan
  • District Co-Head – Blood Heroes (2022–2024)
  • Volunteer – Millions Smiles, Bargad Organization, Alkhidmat Foundation

He is a dynamic civic and youth leader with extensive experience across governance, human rights, environmental advocacy, and community development. He has served with the Global Shapers Community of the World Economic Forum as Social Media Head (2022–2024) and is currently the Curator of the Quetta Hub (2025–2026), where he contributed in flagship environmental initiatives including the Blue & Green Quetta Project, funded by the UGRAD program of the U.S. Consulate General, represented the Quetta Hub at the Global Shapers Annual Summit 2025 in Geneva, Switzerland, initiated Balochistan’s first Youth Policy Project in collaboration with the Human Rights Council of Balochistan and the General Assembly Balochistan, authored the hub’s first Governance Charter, and spearheaded cross-hub collaborations with Global Shapers hubs in Peshawar, Karachi, and Kuala Lumpur. Alongside this, he serves as an Executive Member and Head of the Legal Team at the Human Rights Council of Balochistan, where he works as a volunteer legal advisor representing victims of human rights violations, earned recognition as Best Speaker at the first General Assembly in 2024, and was selected as an Honourable Delegate at the Youth Model National Assembly 2025. His broader engagement includes leadership and volunteer roles as District Co-Head of Blood Heroes (2022–2024), recipient of the Best Performing Volunteer & Social Media Activist Award 2022 from Millions Smiles Pakistan, climate and youth leadership volunteer with Bargad Organization, Cabinet Member of the General Assembly Balochistan (2025), and an active volunteer with the Alkhidmat Foundation and multiple youth-led initiatives, reflecting a sustained commitment to social justice, youth empowerment, and community resilience.

Publications & Research

Mumtaz authored “Human Rights and Responsibilities” (Daily Intekhab, 2021) and a research paper titled “The Role of the Election Commission of Pakistan in Ensuring Free and Fair Elections: Challenges, Mechanisms, and Impact on Democratic Process (2024).” He regularly engages in advocacy, writing, and policy analysis.
